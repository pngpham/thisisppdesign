Type Design

"Radioelectric" Typeface

Description

”Radioelectric” is my novice’s attempt at type revival, which may potentially become a part of the Republish Project’s typeface catalog in the near future. I started some initial sketches since May 2022, and from there, it took me around 4 months to complete the basic character set, special symbols, and Vietnamese support for a total of 3 styles. More time & efforts are still needed to make this typeface ready for public use.

Client

Republish

Scope

Type Design

Year

2022

”Radioelectric” is based on an old electric appliance shop's signage at 239 Tran Hung Dao street, District 1. The original specimen, “Thinh Co” is recognizable for its geometric all-caps type forms, and distinctive inline decorations in every letter. The unique selling point above all, though, is the diacritics—the perfectly rounded horn of the O that resonates really well with the dot above the I.

However, after trials and errors, as well as consultation from my advisors, it was clear to me that the original display letterform might limit the typeface's potential in a digital context. But however hard it might be, I decided to keep the inline construction and continue from there.

Unlike other conventional typefaces, Radioelectric's highly display characteristic challenges the typeface's legibility, especially with Vietnamese diacritics. I took inspiration from the hook above the O in the original specimen being a stripped down dot on the top right corner of the glyph to build a rather condensed set of diacritics and tone marks.

To pay homage to different decorative text styles in Vietnamese vernacular signs, Radioelectric comes in numerous variations, each having its own style of inline decoration. Users can take advantage of this versatility to mix-n-match different styles, creating dynamic combinations.
